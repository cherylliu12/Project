TOP 1. Santa Babara Island

Location: Santa Babara Island-California
Basic Information: Santa Barbara Island is the smallest of the eight Channel Islands, and it is also included in the Channel Islands National Park and Marine Sanctuary. The site is 38 miles off from the California coast. The size of the land is approximately one square mile in area and the highest peak on land is 634 feet. Since the island locates a little far from the coast, it often require divers to stay on charters. Animals: Sea lions, giant sea bass, habor seals and more. Sea lions are the main attraction, and it is very easy to find them since all you need to do is moving calmly and slowly
Visibility: 30-50 feet
best season: Summer and Spring with 80 feet visibility
diving difficulty: beginning

Arch Reef

Located 3/4 miles away directly west of Webster Point. The "Arch" is 55 feet deep and there are walls leading to the home of purple hydrocoral, corynactis anemones, lacy bryozoans, starfish and many more invertebrates. There are also numerous Spiny Lobster located at the top of the "Arch". This is the best place for divers who are interested in smaller creatures.

The Rookery

Located at the island's Southeast side. Divers can try to go below 35 feet but the sand bottom often consist of shallow rockpiles and boulders. However, this is the best spot for meeting California Sea Lions. There are females and pups which loves running into divers and blowing bubbles at them

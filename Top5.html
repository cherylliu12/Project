Top 5.USCG Duane - Key Largo, Florida



Location: Key Largo, Florida
Basic Information: The US Coast Guard "Duane" has the length of 327 feet and served 49 years before its decommission on August 1,1985. The USCG Duane was the oldest US warship at the time when it was decommissioned. Then in 1987, it was sunk in the coast of Key Largo to form an artificial reef. The wreck is currently sitting at a depth of around 120 feet at the deck level. The currents around the wreck can be quite strong from season to season, and this wreck dive falls into the advanced category which is not the best for new divers. Moreover, USCG Duane is much smaller compared to other ship wreck, and this allows divers to explore the entire ship in a single dive.
Marine life: coral, barracuda, turtle, eels and grey nurse sharks
Difficulty: Advanced Open Water
